A Brief History of Computer Viruses

The concept of self-replicating computer programs dates back to the 1940s, but actual viruses didn't appear until decades later.

  1. Creeper (1971): Often considered the first computer virus, created as an experiment on the ARPANET. It displayed the message: "I'm the creeper, catch me if you can!"
  2. Brain (1986): The first PC virus, which infected the boot sector of floppy disks.
  3. ILOVEYOU (2000): A massive email worm that caused billions of dollars in damage globally by overwriting files.
  4. Stuxnet (2010): A highly sophisticated worm designed specifically to sabotage Iranian nuclear facilities.
  5. WannaCry (2017): A global ransomware attack that exploited a vulnerability in older Windows systems.

Modern cyber threats require modern defenses.
