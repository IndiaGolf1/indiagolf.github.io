The History of Computer Viruses

Explore how malicious software evolved from simple experiments into global cyber threats.

Timeline of Malware Evolution

  1. 1971 — Creeper Virus

    The Creeper program is considered one of the first computer worms. It spread between systems on ARPANET and displayed the message: "I’m the creeper, catch me if you can!"

  2. 1986 — Brain Virus

    Created by two brothers in Pakistan, Brain became the first PC boot-sector virus to spread globally.

  3. 1999 — Melissa Virus

    Melissa spread rapidly through email attachments, overloading corporate email servers worldwide.

  4. 2000 — ILOVEYOU Worm

    One of the most destructive worms in history, ILOVEYOU infected millions of computers through email.

  5. 2017 — WannaCry Ransomware

    WannaCry targeted systems worldwide, affecting hospitals, businesses, and government agencies.

Historic Malware Incidents
